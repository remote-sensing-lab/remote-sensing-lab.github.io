I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Geography at Binghamton University, State University of New York ( 纽约州立大学-宾汉姆顿). My research interests focus on Geographical Information Science (GIS), remote sensing, and environmental modeling. More specifically, I am interested in the application of geospatial big data, machine learning, and cloud computing (e.g., Google Earth Engine, AWS, Microsoft Azure) to the study of the physical environment, such as wetland ecosystems and water resources. I am a big fan of open-source GIS. I have created and contributed to various Python packages for advanced geospatial analysis, including lidar, whitebox, WhiteboxTools, and label-maker. Check out my research and blog for more information about what I am currently working on.
